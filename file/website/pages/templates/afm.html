Accounting and Financial management

Accounting and finance management are both crucial aspects of business, but they have distinct focuses. Accounting is primarily concerned with recording, classifying, and summarizing financial transactions to provide a clear picture of a company's financial health. Finance, on the other hand, focuses on the management of a company's monetary resources, including planning, investing, and risk management.
